Activity 04. Professional partner dialogue  

The goals of partner dialogues are to (a) encourage collegiality and (b) provide additional opportunities for professional dialogue with the aim of improving teaching and enhancing professional development. It is important that you select a partner from the same area you are doing your SS, and if you are teaching, preferably someone who teaches the subject “English” with whom to do a partner dialogue in advance, and that they are willing to dedicate the necessary time to this activity. You should also choose a unit from the course you are teaching on your placement to base this activity around in advance, so that necessary plans can be made. If you are not teaching, choose one specific activity you have carried out in as part of your SS to plan this activity.

Instructions

  1. Select a partner (or a colleague from the institution you are rendering your SS) and choose a date and time for your partner dialogue, allocating 30 to 60 minutes to the discussion (you might want to choose a comfortable place and provide refreshments).

Provide your partner with a copy of your unit plan (activity plan if you are not teaching) at least two days prior to your scheduled meeting.

  1. Carry out your lesson and if possible video record it. Ask your coordinator or a person they allocate to carry out an observation of your class, using the observation sheet attached to this activity, (if you are not teaching, create a video that you can share where you explain one of the most insightful activities you are carrying out in your Social Service).
  2. Post-reflection dialogue. Discuss with your partner the results of your teaching/or activity. Use the video if you recorded one.
  3. Post-reflection dialogue report. After having taught this lesson, write a report, incorporating your answers to the questions below:
    • What aspects of the development of your unit plan did you find useful?
    • What did you find was not useful?
    • Suggestions for improvement? Concerns?
    • Do you have suggestions for how we might continue or follow up this work and dialogue?

Include your lesson plans (before and after your dialogue), observation report, post-reflection dialogue report and any other evidence you consider relevant in your e-portfolio.

You can adapt the classroom observation rubric to the specific needs of your teaching context, and if you are not teaching, you can adapt it to an observation rubric that suits the type of activities you are performing.

Once you have done that, post your blog URL in the corresponding activity in Eminus (Professional Partner Dialogue) as a sign you have already complied with the professional partner dialogue activity.

This activity is 2.0 points from your total grade.
