Activity 03. Setting up your e-portfolio  

Instructions: Click on the buttons below to read the information.

It is now time to set up your e-portfolio. As described in the General information, your e-portfolio will take the form of a blog. There are many web services that offer free blog sites, Blogger and WordPress being among the most well-known. Whilst it is up to you to decide which service you use create your e-portfolio, Blogger is one of the simplest (and, therefore, easiest to use) options. Watch Enlace How to create a blog in blogger - Blogger Tutorials. opens a new tab

1 / 3

As said above, your e-portfolio is going to be where you record your Social Service, and it should be something that you work on periodically throughout the semester. In order to aid reflection on your practice during your Social Service placement and to socialise your experiences, two forums will be held with the aim of presenting and discussing issues you are confronting and to provide recommendations for others. After each forum, you should write a brief summary (100-200 words) of the issue you discussed and suggestions you received, and share it in your e-portfolio.

2 / 3

Remember that your e-portfolio is a personal space to record your Social Service, so be as creative as you like in relation to content and presentation, but it must include at least the following:

  1. A presentation of your placement and the objectives of your Social Service there (your action plan, your project outline, and any other information you would like to share to this respect),
  2. the brief summaries resulting from your reflection after participating in the 2 forums,
  3. your professional partner dialogue activity (Section 4),
  4. and a final reflection of the Social Service experience overall.

Our e-portfolios

Instructions

You will now share your blog with the rest of the group. First, post your blog’s URL in the corresponding activity in Eminus (Activity 03. Setting up your e-portfolio), AND then, participate on the corresponding forums to share wit your peers your URL blog and some information about your placement (Blog support forum 01). Later on, you will also have to share with your peers any challenges you have experienced in your placement (Blog support forum 02).

Blog support forum 01 - Your placement  

Instructions

  1. Please participate in the first blog support forum by telling us about your placement and any needs you have identified. You should also take the time to reply to at least two of your peers, sharing knowledge and experience you may already have about similar situations.
  2. When the forum has closed, you should then write up a brief summary of the discussion about your placement and post it on your blog.

Blog support forum 02 - Issues  

Instructions

  1. As with the previous blog support forum, this forum is to provide you with a space to discuss your placement with your peers so as to encourage reflection on your experience.
  2. In this second blog support forum, I would like you to share an issue which you have encountered in your placement, however big or small it may be. Please also reply to at least two of your peers with comments or ideas which could contribute to overcoming the issue they have experienced.
  3. Use the discussion here as a support for writing a reflective post in your blog.
