Section 1. Planning a project

Introduction

In order to develop a project, it is important that certain steps are followed, from identifying an issue or need, to planning how that issue or need can be met or overcome. This section looks at the role of social service and what you need to do in order to plan your Social Service project.

Objectives

The objectives of this section are:

  • To raise awareness of the role of Social Service.
  • To introduce the concept of needs analysis.
  • To carry out a thorough needs analysis process before beginning a project.
  • To develop an appropriate action plan for your Social Service project.

Content

  • Social Service in the BA in ELT.
  • Getting to know your receiving institution.
  • Needs analysis.
  • Action plans.

Your Social Service rendering will give you the opportunity to compensate society for the knowledge you have been cyclically constructing along many years. Getting to know your receiving institution

Now that you have a Social Service placement, it is essential to familiarise yourself with the institution or organization; in order to offer your own skills, you first need to know how the institution works and also, how it doesn’t! The best way to do this is to get talking – to teachers, to students, to directors – and to carry out some observations. This will help you not only to learn about what happens on a daily basis in the institution, but will also allow you to identify if there are any problem areas within the teaching and learning of English.

Needs analysis

Needs analysis refers to the systematic identification of a need. From your chats with different people within the institution and your observations, you may have some hunches about needs that exist there. In order to confirm these hunches, however, it is important that you collect some basic data. The most common way of doing this is through a questionnaire.

Action plans

As we saw above, the final step in a needs analysis is to develop and action plan.

Before you can create an action plan, you need to determine a goal (or goals) that will help to meet the needs identified in your needs analysis process. Relate the needs you have found with what you have learnt during the BA, and speak to your coordinator and other colleagues at your receiving institution in order to determine your goal(s). As we learnt in relation to implementing changes in FOLC, do ensure that your goal is achievable, both in terms of what you, personally, can do, and also the time that is available to you.

Once you have your goal(s), you should identify objectives that will help you to reach it/them. You will then need to propose activities that will meet your objectives. These activities are your actions.
