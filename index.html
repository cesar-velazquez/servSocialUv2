Level of students’ competence

Students write a final paper, where they put together all the skills and knowledge acquired through the BA to a project of their interest, and present it orally to a jury to be examined.

Description

This course, worth 12 credits, has been designed as a guide to help you with the content and writing of your final paper, and focuses mainly on the thesis format. We will begin by looking at the introduction and the points which should be included. This is followed by an in-depth look at the literature review. These sections will be relevant to all students, regardless of the mode in which they decide to present their final paper.

The next two sections of this course will look at the methodology and results chapters of the research paper. Whilst these relate directly to the writing of ‘theses’, students presenting their work in other modes may also want to refer to these sections.​

The following two sections will look at the writing of the discussion chapter, and the conclusion. Just as the first two sections, these sections will be relevant to all students, regardless of the format in which their final paper is presented.

The final section of this course will look at the finishing touches of your final paper. We will look at the writing of abstracts, as well as other basic principles of presentation and more ‘cosmetic’ features that should be taken into consideration when writing academic papers.​

Patricia Nuñez Mercado

Holds a BA in English, a BA in French, a TEFL MA from the University of Veracruz, and a PhD in Applied Linguistics from the University of Southampton. She is a full-time lecturer at the School of Languages of the University of Veracruz, teaching at the BA in English, the online BA in TEFL, the TEFL MA, and the Foreign Language Department. She was the coordinator of the English academy from 2005 to 2011, the Head of the English BA Department from 2011 to 2013, and the coordinator of the tutorial system of the same BA programme from 2013 to 2015. She has supervised numerous students’ research projects in all the programmes she teaches.

She belonged to the Standardized Exams committee of the English BA from 2002 to 2012, and the EXAVER designers’ committee in 2015. She has also taught teacher training courses and workshops for the School of Languages academic staff and has participated in the certification and redesign committees of the BA and MA programmes. She holds a C2 language certification from the University of Cambridge and was a member of the CENEVAL committee for the BA in ELT certification exam. She coordinates the Research Group Applied Linguistics in English Language Teaching and Learning (UVU V-CA-543) and is a member of the RECALE (a national network for research groups in foreign languages). Her research interests are L2 literacy in higher education, English language learning and teaching, and research processes. She has participated in numerous national and international conferences, both as a presenter and attendee. She has published several book chapters and journal articles, coordinated several books on the ELT training area, and co-authored a research report writing handbook for beginners.


Daisy Giles

Daisy Giles graduated with Distinction from the MA in Hispanic Studies, with a module in Teaching English as a Foreign Language, at the University of Glasgow.

She also holds an MSc in Digital Education (with Distinction) from the University of Edinburgh. She started her teaching career as a British Council Language Assistant at the Pontífica Universidad Católica de Ecuador, followed by a further British Council Assistantship at the Universidad Veracruzana, where she taught for over 12 years, both in the BA in English Language and the online BA in English Language Teaching. In addition to teaching, she has also had roles with EXAVER and the Department of Standardised Exams of the BA in English Language. She has collaborated in a number of research projects on topics related to academic literacies, student research and online education, having frequently participated as a conference presenter and published book chapters and journal articles. From 2017 to 2019 she served as Head of Programme for the online BA in English Language Teaching, during which time she coordinated the evaluation process which lead to the BA being certified as a Programme of Quality by COAPEHUM, as well as a curriculum and syllabus evaluation of the same programme, leading to its redesign. She currently lives in Portugal, where she works online and is involved in several projects related to the teaching of English and Portuguese.

Reyna Isidra López Blé

Reyna Isidra López Blé studied the BA in English and the BA in French at the University of Veracruz. She graduated with Distinction from the MA in Teaching English as a Foreign Language and she also obtained the MA in French Didactics at the same university. She has been a teacher of English and French in Higher Education for more than 12 years. At the moment, she teaches a variety of courses at the BA in English, the BA in French and the Online BA in ELT at Universidad Veracruzana. She holds a C2 level English certification by the University of Cambridge and a C2 level French Certification.

She is an EXAVER (1, 2, and 3) examiner. She participated in the certification process of the BA in ELT and she is part of the redesign committee of the same programme. She has supervised a number of undergraduate and graduate research papers and collaborates with the Research Group Lingüística Aplicada a la Enseñanza y Aprendizaje del Inglés (CA UVU V 543). She has participated as a presenter and attendee in numerous national conferences and has published book chapters and journal articles. She is interested in research on EFL Academic Writing, Academic Writing and Identity, Systemic Functional Linguistics and Language Teaching and Learning processes, amongst others. Close
